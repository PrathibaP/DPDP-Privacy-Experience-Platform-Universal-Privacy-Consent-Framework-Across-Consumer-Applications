Product Thinking

From one bundled checkbox to a trust asset

The reasoning behind the redesign — vision, research, the gap in today's experience, and the four-pillar structure that replaces it.

Vision

Privacy at Zomato should feel like a feature you'd brag about, not a policy you tolerate. Every person ordering food should see, in plain language, exactly what we know, why we know it, and how to change their mind — in under three taps.

Mission

Replace one bundled consent checkbox with a purpose-wise, self-serve Privacy Hub that satisfies the DPDP Act end-to-end, while measurably improving trust, retention and personalization opt-in.

Opportunity

Zomato collects location, payment, order-history and communication data across Zomato, Blinkit and District. Today's consent is one bundled acceptance at signup, with settings buried three levels deep — while DPDP, rising data-sensitivity, and manual support tickets converge into one problem.

About This Project

Product philosophy & how this was built

Product philosophy

  • Consent is a conversation, not a checkbox.
  • Every control a user can see, they can also change.
  • Compliance is the floor, not the ceiling — trust is the product.
  • Self-serve first: rights requests resolve in hours, not the 30-day statutory ceiling.

Design principles

  • Purpose-wise over bundled — one toggle, one purpose.
  • Live, not static — every claim is computed from real state.
  • Undo on every save — lowers the cost of experimenting with a toggle.
  • One design system, tokens-first — dark mode proves it, not just describes it.

Engineering principles

  • One Consent Service and one Audit stream shared across Zomato, Blinkit, District.
  • Event-driven backbone — new consumers never require producer changes.
  • Server-side field filtering — partner APIs need no contract change.
  • Feature-flagged screens — a regression in one never forces a full rollback.

This case-study site itself

  • Single self-contained HTML file — no build step, no external app dependency.
  • Semantic HTML, visible focus states, reduced-motion support, keyboard-navigable.
  • Vanilla HTML / CSS / JavaScript — the interactive prototype embedded above is the real file, sandboxed live.
  • Fully responsive — desktop, laptop, tablet, and mobile, portrait and landscape.

01 · The Story

Five beats, one reframe.

Compliance work is usually a cost centre. Here's the arc that turns it into the reason parents choose — and stay with — Vedantu.

Problem

One checkbox, every user.

Signup bundles ToS, privacy, and marketing consent into a single "Accept & Continue" tap — with no distinction between an 18-year-old JEE aspirant and a 13-year-old signing up with a parent's phone.

Insight

Parents decide trust. Students decide friction tolerance.

A 2024 LocalCircles survey found 76% of parents want visibility into what ed-tech apps do with their child's data — but any added friction risks suppressing the signup and activation metrics Vedantu depends on.

Opportunity

Turn a legal obligation into a trust narrative.

DPDP Sec. 9 requires verifiable parental consent for any user under 18 — Vedantu's core demographic. Meeting that bar visibly, instead of quietly, becomes a differentiator competitors treating privacy as boilerplate can't match.

Solution

The Privacy Hub — five pillars, one home.

Verifiable parent consent, contextual (not bundled) permission requests, granular purpose-scoped controls, a full consent audit timeline, and proactive transparency on retention and AI use.

Impact

95%+ valid consent, without breaking activation.

Measured against a North Star of DPDP-valid consent coverage, with explicit guardrails so verification friction never erodes signup conversion or D7 activation beyond a defined threshold.

02 · The Problem

Where today's flow breaks DPDP — and breaks trust.

A heuristic walkthrough of the current signup-to-classroom journey shows five places where the product is silent when the law — and the parent — expects it to speak up.

Before

Bundled, invisible, one-way

  • DOB collected at signup, but no branch logic for under-18 accounts.
  • Camera/mic requested by the OS only — no in-app purpose shown first.
  • WhatsApp, push, and marketing email bundled with essential class alerts.
  • Deletion or export only via emailing support — no SLA, no confirmation.
After

Age-aware, contextual, revocable

  • Under-18 accounts branch into OTP-based parent verification before any class or AI feature activates.
  • Camera/mic explained in plain language at first live-class join — not buried at signup.
  • Marketing, analytics, and AI recommendations each get their own toggle.
  • Self-serve export and delete, with a 7-day recoverable grace window and a receipt.

04 · The Solution

The Privacy Hub — one place to see, control, and revoke every permission.

Six features, each mapped to a DPDP obligation and an engineering owner. Tap a card for the full breakdown.

01

Age-Gated Onboarding

DOB branches under-18 users into OTP-based parent verification before any class or AI feature activates.

Consent History Log

Every grant or revoke is timestamped and visible to both student and parent — the audit trail DPDP Sec. 8 expects.

07 · Rollout Plan

Phased rollout — with a hard line on what never gets A/B tested.

Verification friction gets tested carefully. Whether consent is required at all does not.

Phase 0

Legal + Dogfood

  • DPDP counsel signs off on consent copy & retention logic
  • Internal team tests the full flow end-to-end
Phase 1

5% New-Signup Beta

  • New users only, one geography
  • Monitor OTP delivery, verification rate, support volume
Phase 2

Gradual Rollout

  • Expand to 100% of new signups
  • Begin scheduled re-consent prompts for existing under-18 accounts
Phase 3

Full Migration

  • All existing accounts re-verified
  • Legacy bundled-consent flow retired
Never A/B tested
  • Whether parental consent is required for under-18 accounts — a legal obligation, not a growth lever.
  • Whether a deletion or export request gets executed — every valid request is honored, no experiment arms.
  • Core disclosures (what data, why, retention period) — copy tone can be tested, the facts disclosed cannot.

09 · About this case study

Built as a portfolio piece — treated like a real launch.

P

Prathiba P

Aspiring Product Manager

This case study was built as a Product Management internship assignment, then extended into a full PRD and interactive prototype to show product thinking end-to-end — from problem framing to a feature a legal counsel could actually sign off on.

Biggest challenge

Balancing legal necessity against product friction — parent verification is non-negotiable under Sec. 9, but every added step risks signup conversion. The guardrail metrics in Section 5 of the PRD exist specifically to keep that tension visible.

What I'd do differently

Validate the OTP-verification flow with parents in low-connectivity regions earlier — the accessibility risk (not every household has an actively-checked parent phone) surfaced late in the process.

What's next

Architecture and system-design views, a live consent-health dashboard, and a presenter-mode reader for the deck and PRD — shipping in the next iteration of this site.
